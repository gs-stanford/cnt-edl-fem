CNT Bundle EDL FEM Explorer

General nonlinear Poisson-Boltzmann around same-bias CNT cylinders, compared against the Debye-Huckel limit.

Geometry

Bias and Electrolyte

Applied metal voltage is partitioned across a compact Stern layer and the diffuse PB layer.

The species coefficients multiply c0. Example: a 2:1 salt uses c+=1 and c-=2. Bulk electroneutrality is required.

Numerics

Boundary model

Current version prescribes metal voltage on each CNT and uses a compact Stern capacitance as a Robin boundary condition in the PB/FEM solve.

Model equations

The solver computes the Debye length from the selected electrolyte:

λD = κ-1,   κ2 = e2NA ϵrϵ0kBT c0 ∑izi2ci

In the calculation, c0 is entered in mM; numerically, 1 mM = 1 mol m-3.

Then it solves the general nondimensional nonlinear PB equation:

∇*2Ψ* = - ∑iziciexp(-ziΨ*) ∑izi2ci

The applied CNT metal voltage is partitioned through a compact Stern layer before the FEM solve:

Ψm* = Ψd* + δSqS*,   δS = ϵrϵ0 CSλD

This version imposes the Stern relation as a Robin boundary term in the FEM solve, so local surface charge and local Stern drop can vary around each CNT.

with Stern-partitioned Ψd* on all CNT boundaries and Ψ*=0 at the outer bath boundary. The DH comparison is the small-potential limit ∇*2Ψ*=Ψ*.

For a single cylinder, the website also checks the FEM Debye-Huckel charge against the analytic cylindrical DH reference Ψ*(r*)=A K0(r*) with the same Stern Robin boundary condition. Nonlinear cylindrical PB is still solved numerically; it does not have the simple planar Gouy-Chapman closed form.
